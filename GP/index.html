Schoolboy, 7, youngest in history to present petition to Scottish Parliament

The Livingston schoolboy will become the youngest person to ever hand over a petition to the Scottish Parliament.

A schoolboy will make his mark in history today when he becomes the youngest person ever to present a petition before the Scottish Parliament. Seven-year-old Callum Isted will hand over his case to politicians this afternoon for every primary school pupil in Scotland to receive a reusable water bottle.;

The youngster's campaign follows his own success after he raised cash to buy refillable bottles for his fellow schoolmates at Dedridge Primary School in Livingston. Keen to make a difference, he also said he wants to take action to fix broken taps in schools "so that we can use the bottles properly”.

31-year-old rushed to hospital with head injuries following violent attack on Sunday afternoon

Victim was attacked at 3pm on Sunday and police have issued a description of his attacker

A man had to be rushed to hospital for head injuries after being attacked in broad daylight on Sunday afternoon. Police are appealing for information following the serious assault on Deans North Road, Livingston, around 3pm and have issued a description of the suspect. A 31-year-old man was walking on a footpath behind a bus stop opposite Raeburn Rigg on Deans North Road, when he was approached by a man. He sustained injuries to his head and body and was taken to St John’s Hospital for treatment. The suspect is described as being around five feet seven in height, of stocky build, bald and clean shaven. He was wearing a light blue t-shirt, blue jeans and white shoes. It is believed the suspect was driving a black coloured people carrier.
